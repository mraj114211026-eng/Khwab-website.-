About the Event

"Khwab" — A Dream Awakened

Khwab symbolizes the birth of a dream — a platform where ideas take flight and voices find their purpose. This college-level debate competition by the Khwab Committee invites budding legal minds to articulate perspectives on compelling social and legal questions of our time.

Through structured argument and persuasive advocacy, participants will sharpen their analytical ability, refine their legal reasoning, and build the confidence to stand before any bench.


About Khwab Committee

The Khwab Committee is the academic and intellectual wing of Vedanta Law College, actively promoting legal awareness, research, advocacy skills, and public speaking through debates, discussions, and seminars — cultivating leadership in budding legal minds.

Debate plays a vital role in legal education as it sharpens analytical ability, nurtures clarity of thought, and instills confidence in expression. Such academic initiatives help students evolve into responsible legal professionals and informed citizens. We extend a warm welcome to all participants.

Competition Structure

Format & Rounds

R1
Preliminary Round
3 + 1 Minutes

Participants present arguments with focus on content (legal/factual accuracy), clarity, citation of authorities, and overall presentation quality.

R2
Semi-Final Round
3 + 2 Minutes

First 3 minutes for core presentation; final 2 minutes dedicated exclusively to rebuttals. The art of dismantling opposing arguments takes center stage.

R3
Grand Final
2 Minutes + Q&A

Finalists deliver a powerful summation of their arguments, followed by direct questions from the panel of judges — the ultimate test of advocacy.

⚠ Non-adherence to allotted time limits will attract penalties.

Conduct Guidelines

Rules of Conduct

✓ The Do's

1

Strictly adhere to all allotted time limits across every round without exception.

2

Use professional language and support all arguments with sound legal principles and cited authorities.

3

Mandatory: Attendance in proper college uniform throughout the duration of the competition.

✗ The Don'ts

1

Use of unparliamentary, aggressive, or defamatory language of any kind is strictly prohibited.

2

Use of mobile phones or any electronic gadgets during the rounds is not permitted.

3

Personal attacks on opponents or interrupting other speakers while they hold the floor.
